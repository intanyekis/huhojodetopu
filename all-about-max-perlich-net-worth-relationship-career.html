How did Max Perlich start his Professional Career?

Perlich made his acting debut in the 1985 short drama Dummies. The following year, he was cast as Anderson in the American teen comedy film Ferris Bueller’s Day Off.

Perlich went on to appear in a variety of films, including The Allnighter, Can’t Buy Me Love, Plain Clothes, Vibes, Drugstore Cowboy, Horseplayer, Cliffhanger, Cityscrapes: Los Angeles, Homeward Bound II: Lost in San Francisco, and others.

From 1995 to 1997, he starred as J.H. Brodie in the American police procedural television series Homicide: Life on the Street, with Melissa Leo, Jon Seda, Callie Thorne, and others. Perlich’s other television credits include Buffy the Vampire Slayer, Fantasy Island, Todd McFarlane’s Spawn, Gilmore Girls, Crossing Jordan, Charmed, and Justified.
