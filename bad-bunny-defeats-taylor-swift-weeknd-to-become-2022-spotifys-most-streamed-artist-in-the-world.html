Bad Bunny defeats Taylor Swift, Weeknd, to become 2022 Spotifys most-streamed artist in the world

The top spot for the 2022 Spotify’s most-streamed artist in the world has been claimed by Bad Bunny, surpassing Taylor Swift and The Weeknd. A picture of Bad Bunny with the article has been provided. This marks the third consecutive year of Bad Bunny’s global dominance on Spotify.

Bad Bunny’s album “Un Verano Sin Ti” has been nominated for the upcoming Grammys and has also been declared as Spotify’s most-streamed album worldwide for 2022. Spotify has announced a special honor for Bad Bunny by changing its heart icon to the design from the album art.

In the US, Drake holds the title of the most-streamed artist of the year, followed by Taylor Swift. The top five also includes Kanye West, despite ongoing controversies related to his recent comments.

Harry Styles has achieved a significant feat with his single “As It Was” from the Grammy-nominated album “Harry’s House” being the most-streamed song of the year worldwide.

Bad Bunny’s tracks “Me Porto Bonito” and “Tití Me Preguntó” have secured the fourth and fifth positions in the worldwide top five tracks list.

The most-streamed artists globally include Bad Bunny, Taylor Swift, Drake, The Weeknd, and BTS. In the US, the top artists on Spotify are Drake, Taylor Swift, Bad Bunny, Kanye West, and The Weeknd.

The most-streamed albums globally are “Un Verano Sin Ti” by Bad Bunny, “Harry’s House” by Harry Styles, “SOUR” by Olivia Rodrigo, “=” by Ed Sheeran, and “Planet Her” by Doja Cat.

The list of the most-streamed songs globally includes “As It Was” by Harry Styles, “Heat Waves” by Glass Animals, “STAY (with Justin Bieber)” by The Kid LAROI, “Me Porto Bonito” by Bad Bunny feat. Chencho Corleon, and “Tití Me Preguntó” by Bad Bunny.

In the US, the most-streamed songs are “As It Was” by Harry Styles, “Heat Waves” by Glass Animals, “Bad Habit” by Steve Lacy, “Me Porto Bonito” by Bad Bunny feat. Chencho Corleon, and “First Class” by Jack Harlow.
