Nicola Yoon Biography-Wiki

Nicola Yoon is a Jamaican-American author. She is well recognized for writing the 2015 young adult novel Everything. Everything is a New York Times bestseller which is the basis of a 2017 film of the same name.

Yoon grew up in Jamaica and in Brooklyn, New York. As an undergraduate at Cornell University, she majored in electrical engineering.

She was also taking a creative writing class as an elective and it got her hooked on writing. After graduating, Yoon attended the Master of Creative Writing program at Emerson College.

Nicola Yoon Writing

For 20 years, before the publication of her first book, Yoon worked as a programmer for investment management firms. Yoon is associated with the We Need Diverse Books organization, which promotes the representation of diversity in literature.

She was a contributor to Because You Love to Hate Me, an anthology of short stories written by 13 YA authors who were paired with 13 BookTubers, who provided writing prompts. It was published in July 2017.

Nicola Yoon Everything, Everything

Her debut novel, Everything, Everything, was inspired by the birth of her biracial daughter. She wanted to write a book that reflected her child on the pages.

Yoon’s first-time mother worries about protecting her baby from danger gave her the idea to write a story about a 17-year-old girl who needed the same level of protection.

The book took her three years to write; writing early in the mornings while working full-time and raising her infant daughter. The illustrations were drawn by her Korean American graphic designer David Yoon.

The book was s released in September 2015 and debuted as No. 1 New York Times bestseller for young adult hardcover books. It spent 40 weeks on the bestseller list.

A film of the same name based on the book, adapted by J. Mills Goodloe and starring Amandla Stenberg and Nick Robinson, was released in May 2017.

Nicola Yoon The Sun Is Also a Star

In November 2016, Yoon’s second book, The Sun Is Also a Star, was released. This one too reached number one on the New York Times Best Seller list.

The book was a 2016 National Book Award finalist. It was included in The New York Times Book Review list of Notable Children’s Books of 2016. It was also listed in the top 10 books of 2016 by Entertainment Weekly and the Los Angeles Times.
